Called Up exists to unite Christian baseball players, disciple them in what it looks like to follow Jesus, and send them back into their locker rooms with the gospel.

Why Called Up Exists

Make Disciples Who Make Disciples.

Called Up was born out of a real need: wanting to follow Jesus in college baseball, but feeling alone, burnt out, and unsure how faith connected to the purpose of playing.

We create space for baseball players to be discipled through Scripture, gospel conversations, and practical lessons on loving God, loving others, and living as witnesses on their teams.

The goal is not just community for community's sake. The goal is to see the gospel go forth into locker rooms, organizations, local churches, and the nations.

The Weekly Call

Are You Making Disciples?

The weekly call is a starting point for discipleship. We open the Word, learn from faithful leaders and guest speakers, pray for our teams, and talk through what obedience to Jesus looks like right now.

The aim is simple: help players grow as disciples, learn to lead a Discovery Bible Study, and carry the gospel into their own teams with humility and courage.

  • Scripture and prayer with other players
  • Honest conversation about faith and baseball
  • Practical training to disciple teammates

Common Questions

Simple Next Steps.

Who can join?

Any college baseball player who wants to follow Jesus, grow with other believers, and learn to make disciples on his team.

What happens after I sign up?

You'll get a welcome email with the GroupMe link. The call link and next steps will be shared there.

Do I need to attend every call?

No. Show up as consistently as you can. The goal is steady discipleship that fits the rhythm of a baseball season.
